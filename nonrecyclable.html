Non-Recyclable Plastics

Non-recyclable plastics are plastics that are unable to be recycled. They are usually sent to landfills as there is no real way to properly dispose of them. One possible solution is to deliver the non-recyclabe plastic to a recycling center. Non-recyclable plastics being put into landfills cause harsh chemicals to be released and harm the surrounding environment. For instance, they can pollute oceans, lakes, and rivers. Some widely used non-recyclable plastics include cling film, bubble wrap, potato chip bags, water bottle caps, and more. It is evident that non-recyclable plastics are prevalent in our everyday lives yet they detrimentally impact the environment.
